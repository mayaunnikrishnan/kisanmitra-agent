# Government Agricultural Schemes — Eligibility Reference

This file contains simplified eligibility information for major central government
agricultural schemes in India. Use this to match a farmer's profile (state, land size,
crop, farmer category) against scheme conditions.

---

## 1. PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi)

**What it offers:** Direct income support of ₹6,000 per year, paid in three
installments of ₹2,000 each, directly to the farmer's bank account.

**Eligibility:**
- All landholding farmer families (small and marginal farmers prioritized)
- Land must be in the farmer's name (or family member's name) in land records
- Excludes: institutional landholders, and farmer families where any member is a
  serving/retired government employee above a certain pay grade, income tax payee,
  or professional (doctor, engineer, lawyer, CA) practicing professionally

**Next step:** Apply via the PM-KISAN portal (pmkisan.gov.in) or through the local
revenue/agriculture department office. Requires land records and Aadhaar-linked bank account.

---

## 2. PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana)

**What it offers:** Crop insurance against losses due to natural calamities, pests,
and diseases. Farmer pays a small subsidized premium (2% for Kharif crops, 1.5% for
Rabi crops, 5% for commercial/horticultural crops); the government subsidizes the rest.

**Eligibility:**
- All farmers growing notified crops in notified areas, including both loanee
  (those who've taken crop loans) and non-loanee farmers
- Both landowner and tenant farmers/sharecroppers are eligible
- Crop must be on the list of notified crops for that season and state

**Next step:** Apply through banks, Common Service Centres (CSC), insurance company
portals, or the national crop insurance portal (pmfby.gov.in), within the enrollment
window before the crop season starts.

---

## 3. KCC (Kisan Credit Card)

**What it offers:** Short-term credit for farming needs (seeds, fertilizer,
equipment) at subsidized interest rates, with interest subvention making effective
rates as low as 4% per annum for loans up to ₹3 lakh (with timely repayment).

**Eligibility:**
- All farmers — landowners, tenant farmers, oral lessees, sharecroppers
- Includes individual or joint borrowers, and self-help groups/joint liability
  groups of farmers
- No strict minimum land size requirement

**Next step:** Apply at any nationalized bank, regional rural bank, or cooperative
bank branch. Requires land documents (or tenancy proof) and identity/address proof.

---

## 4. PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan)

**What it offers:** Subsidy (typically 30–50% of cost, varies by component and state,
with additional state-level top-up in some cases) for installing solar-powered
irrigation pumps, reducing dependence on diesel pumps and grid electricity.

**Eligibility:**
- Individual farmers, farmer groups/cooperatives, or panchayats
- Must have access to suitable land for installing the solar pump/plant
- Priority often given to areas without reliable grid electricity access

**Next step:** Apply through the state renewable energy agency (e.g., state nodal
agency for renewable energy) or via the PM-KUSUM portal where available for that state.

---

## 5. PMKSY — Per Drop More Crop (Micro-Irrigation Component)

**What it offers:** Subsidy of 55% (general category) to 80% (small/marginal/
SC-ST farmers in many states) of the cost of drip or sprinkler irrigation systems.

**Eligibility:**
- Any farmer wishing to install drip/sprinkler irrigation on their land
- Small and marginal farmers, and SC/ST farmers, generally receive a higher
  subsidy percentage than general category farmers
- Exact subsidy slab varies by state — this reference treats it as a general
  approximate guide, not the final word

**Next step:** Apply through the state agriculture or horticulture department,
or empanelled micro-irrigation companies registered under the scheme in that state.

---

## 6. SMAM (Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization)

**What it offers:** Subsidy (typically 40–50% of cost, higher for small/marginal
and SC/ST/women farmers) on farm machinery — tractors, power tillers, harvesters,
seed drills, and other equipment.

**Eligibility:**
- Individual farmers, farmer groups, cooperatives, or Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs)
- Small, marginal, women, and SC/ST farmers typically receive higher subsidy
  percentages than general category farmers
- Some equipment subsidies require the farmer not already own that category of
  machinery (to avoid duplication)

**Next step:** Apply through the state agriculture department or via the Direct
Benefit Transfer in Agriculture Mechanization (DBT) portal where active for that state.

---

## Notes on Using This Reference

- Subsidy percentages and amounts above are approximate and meant for general
  guidance only — they can vary by state, year, and budget allocation.
- This file does not cover state-specific schemes, which are numerous and vary
  significantly by state.
- Always direct the farmer to confirm final details at the official portal or
  local agriculture office before assuming eligibility is final.
